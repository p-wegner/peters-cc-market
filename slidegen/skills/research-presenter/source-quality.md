## Source Evaluation Framework

### Authority Assessment
**Questions to ask**:
1. **Who created this content?** - Credentials, expertise, institutional backing
2. **What is their expertise?** - Education, experience, recognition in field
3. **What institutional support?** - University, company, organization resources
4. **What is their track record?** - Previous work, citations, reputation

**Authority Indicators**:
âœ… **Advanced degrees** in relevant field
âœ… **Professional experience** (5+ years preferred)
âœ… **Peer recognition** and citations
âœ… **Institutional affiliation** with reputable organization
âœ… **Publication history** in quality venues

### Accuracy Verification
**Questions to ask**:
1. **Can information be verified?** - Cross-reference with other sources
2. **Is methodology clear?** - How data was collected and analyzed
3. **Are claims supported?** - Evidence for each assertion
4. **Are corrections handled?** - How errors are addressed

**Accuracy Indicators**:
âœ… **Multiple source confirmation** for key facts
âœ… **Clear methodology** explanation
âœ… **Data sources** properly cited
âœ… **Error correction** policies
âœ… **Regular updates** to maintain accuracy

### Currency Evaluation
**Questions to ask**:
1. **When was this published?** - Publication and update dates
2. **Is information still relevant?** - Changes since publication
3. **Are there newer sources?** - More recent research available
4. **Is the field fast-changing?** - Rapid development areas need recent sources

**Currency Standards**:

| Field Type | Preferred Age | Acceptable Age | Outdated Beyond |
|------------|----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| Technology | 6 months | 12 months | 24 months |
| Business/Finance | 12 months | 24 months | 36 months |
| Science/Research | 24 months | 36 months | 60 months |
| Policy/Regulation | 6 months | 12 months | 24 months |
| Historical Analysis | 5-10 years | 15 years | 25 years |

### Purpose and Bias Analysis
**Questions to ask**:
1. **Why was this created?** - Educational, commercial, persuasive intent
2. **Who funded it?** - Potential conflicts of interest
3. **What perspective is presented?** - Balanced or viewpoint-specific
4. **Are multiple views included?** - Recognition of alternative perspectives

**Bias Indicators**:
âœ… **Funding disclosure** - Transparent sponsor information
âœ… **Multiple perspectives** - Inclusion of counterarguments
âœ… **Balanced language** - Avoidance of loaded terms
âœ… **Acknowledged limitations** - Honest discussion of constraints
âœ… **Professional tone** - Objective, analytical approach

## Source Integration Best Practices

### Citation Standards
#### In-Presentation Citations
```markdown
### Statistical Data
"Remote work increased by 159% between 2020-2023"
*Source: Stanford Workforce of the Future Survey, 2024*

### Expert Opinion
"AI integration will transform healthcare delivery within 5 years"
- Dr. Sarah Chen, Chief Medical Officer, HealthTech Institute
*Harvard Medical School, 2024 Research Symposium*

### Market Research
"The global AI market is projected to reach $1.8 trillion by 2030"
*McKinsey Global Institute, "The State of AI in 2024"*
```

#### Source Documentation
```markdown
## Research Sources

### Primary Sources
1. **Government Data**
   - Bureau of Labor Statistics. "Remote Work Statistics." Q2 2024.
   - Department of Commerce. "Digital Economy Report." 2024.

2. **Academic Research**
   - Smith, J. et al. "Productivity in Distributed Teams." *Nature*, vol. 15, no. 3, 2024.
   - Johnson, M. "AI Implementation in Healthcare." *New England Journal of Medicine*, 2024.

### Industry Reports
1. **McKinsey & Company**
   - "The Future of Work After COVID-19." March 2024.
   - "AI in Healthcare: Adoption and Impact." June 2024.

2. **Gartner Research**
   - "Hype Cycle for Artificial Intelligence." July 2024.
   - "Market Guide for Remote Work Technology." May 2024.
```

### Data Presentation Standards

#### Statistical Claims
**Requirements for statistical presentations**:
- **Specific numbers**: Use exact figures rather than vague statements
- **Time context**: Include relevant time periods
- **Source attribution**: Clear citation of data source
- **Methodology note**: Brief explanation of how data was collected
- **Comparison points**: Provide context for interpretation

**Example**:
```markdown
### Remote Work Growth

<div class="stat-highlight">

**159% Increase** in remote work prevalence
*2020-2023 period*

</div>

*Methodology: Survey of 2,500 companies across 15 industries*
*Source: Stanford Workforce of the Future Survey, 2024*
```

#### Trend Analysis
**Requirements for trend presentations**:
- **Historical context**: Baseline data for comparison
- **Causal factors**: Explanation of drivers behind trends
- **Expert validation**: Support from industry experts
- **Future projections**: Carefully labeled forecasts
- **Confidence intervals**: Statistical confidence in projections

**Example**:
```markdown
### AI Adoption Trends

<div class="trend-data">

**2020**: 15% of companies using AI
**2022**: 35% of companies using AI
**2024**: 58% of companies using AI

*Projected 2026: 75% of companies using AI*

</div>

*Key drivers: Improved technology accessibility, demonstrated ROI, competitive pressure*
*Expert validation: Confirmed by MIT Technology Review and Gartner analysis*
```

## Quality Assurance Process

### Pre-Integration Checks
Before including research in presentations:

#### Source Verification
- [ ] **Authority confirmed**: Source creator has relevant expertise
- [ ] **Publication verified**: Source is legitimate and properly published
- [ ] **Date verified**: Information is current and relevant
- [ ] **Bias assessed**: Potential conflicts identified and addressed
- [ ] **Methodology reviewed**: Data collection methods are sound

#### Content Validation
- [ ] **Cross-referenced**: Key facts confirmed with multiple sources
- [ ] **Context understood**: Information placed in appropriate context
- [ ] **Limitations noted**: Constraints and uncertainties acknowledged
- [ ] **Relevance confirmed**: Information directly supports presentation goals
- [ ] **Clarity ensured**: Complex information explained clearly

### Post-Integration Review
After including research in presentations:

#### Accuracy Check
- [ ] **Proper attribution**: All sources correctly cited
- [ ] **No misrepresentation**: Information not taken out of context
- [ ] **Balanced perspective**: Multiple viewpoints included when relevant
- [ ] **Current status**: Information still up-to-date at time of presentation
- [ ] **Professional presentation**: Research integrated smoothly and professionally

### Verification Strategies

#### Cross-Reference Process
1. **Primary source identification**: Locate original research or data
2. **Secondary source confirmation**: Find additional reputable sources
3. **Expert validation**: Check what recognized experts say on the topic
4. **Contradictory evidence**: Look for conflicting information
5. **Consensus assessment**: Determine general agreement among experts

#### Quality Scoring System
Rate sources on a 1-5 scale for each dimension:

| Dimension | 5 (Excellent) | 3 (Acceptable) | 1 (Poor) |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|----------|
| **Authority** | Leading expert institution | Professional organization | Unknown source |
| **Accuracy** | Verified by multiple sources | Generally reliable | Unverified claims |
| **Currency** | Published within 6 months | Within 2-3 years | Over 5 years old |
| **Objectivity** | Balanced, multiple perspectives | Some bias evident | Strong bias/agenda |
| **Methodology** | Transparent, rigorous | Generally sound | Unclear/weak |

**Minimum score for inclusion**: 12 out of 20 points

## Special Considerations

### Industry-Specific Standards

#### Healthcare and Medical Research
- **Peer-reviewed journals** required for clinical claims
- **FDA/EMA approval** status for medical treatments
- **Clinical trial data** with proper controls and sample sizes
- **Professional medical guidelines** from established associations
- **Conflicts of interest** must be fully disclosed

#### Financial and Economic Data
- **Regulatory filings** for public company information
- **Government economic statistics** for macro data
- **Audited financial statements** for company performance
- **Central bank data** for monetary and economic indicators
- **Established financial databases** for market information

#### Technology and Engineering
- **Technical papers** from peer-reviewed conferences and journals
- **Patent filings** and technical documentation
- **Standards organizations** specifications and certifications
- **Open source repositories** with community validation
- **Industry technical standards** bodies

#### Social Science and Policy
- **Academic research** with proper methodology
- **Government statistics** and policy documents
- **Think tank research** with transparent funding
- **Survey research** with proper sampling methodology
- **Historical documents** with proven authenticity
